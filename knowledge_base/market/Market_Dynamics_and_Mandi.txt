Market Dynamics, APMC Mandi Rates, and Supply Chain Guidelines
==============================================================

AgriTrace connects farmers directly to national commodity channels and tracks logistics compliance.

1. APMC MANDI RATES & eNAM ARBITRAGE
------------------------------------
- Commodities are traded across APMC (Agricultural Produce Market Committee) mandis. eNAM (electronic National Agriculture Market) integrates these mandis into a single electronic screen.
- Price Arbitrage: Farmers utilize market analysis metrics to compare peak rates and average rates per quintal across states, routing produce to districts with highest demand.
- Price Trends: Market price trends are categorized as UPWARD, DOWNWARD, or STABLE based on rolling seasonal arrivals and supply volume metrics.

2. STORAGE TEMPERATURE & HUMIDITY STANDARDS
-------------------------------------------
- Grains (Rice/Wheat): Must be dried to under 14% moisture content to prevent mold, insect infestation, and spoilage. Ideal storage: relative humidity 60-65% at 20-25°C.
- Potatoes: Store in cold storage at 4-7°C with high relative humidity (90%) to prevent weight loss, rot, and sprouting.
- Banana & Perishables: Store in well-ventilated ripening chambers with controlled ethylene gas and temperature levels (13-15°C).

3. TRANSPORTATION & COLD CHAIN COMPLIANCE
-----------------------------------------
- Perishables (Tomato, Banana) require continuous refrigerated transport (Reefer Logistics) to prevent heat stress and shelf-life reduction.
- First-In, First-Out (FIFO) batch movements ensure stock turnover matching harvest dates.
- Data handoffs are cryptographically signed and tracked on-chain via batch QR codes.
